I'm a fourth-year Ph.D. student in linguistics at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). My main interest is semantics, and I have worked on topics such as tense, aspect, modality, degrees, and attitude reports through fieldwork and experiments.

Currently, I primarily work on Gitksan (Tsimshianic) and Japanese, and I have also worked on American Sign Language (ASL). I am part of the Gitksan Research Lab at University of British Columbia (UBC) and Meaning and Modality Lab at Harvard University.

I have a B.A. and an M.A. from UBC. My M.A. thesis was on tense semantics in Gitksan, Japanese, and English. My B.A. Honours thesis was on adjective intensification in ASL.
